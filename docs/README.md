Our goal is to accelerate onboarding, standardize repeatable workflows, reduce knowledge silos, and enable every team member to access validated, living process guidance.

## Project Management Processes — Overview

OctoAcme manages projects using a structured lifecycle of Initiation, Planning, Execution & Tracking, Release, and Retrospective. In the **Initiation phase**, teams define business needs in a one-pager, clarify stakeholders, set measurable outcomes, and assess initial risks and resources. The **Planning phase** turns these into actionable, prioritized backlog items—each with acceptance criteria—maps out dependencies, and aligns on milestones and test strategies.

**Execution** is driven by a disciplined cadence: daily standups to surface blockers, weekly delivery syncs, and sprint-based reviews ensure strong operational focus. Teams track work via a project board (Backlog → Ready → In Progress → In Review → QA → Done), enforce small and testable pull requests, and require at least one code review approval. Quality is built in through layered testing (unit, integration, end-to-end), CI, security scanning, and manual QA for releases. Success metrics, velocity, and risks are visible through dashboards, while escalation follows three levels: team, PM/Product Lead, and sponsor.

Transparency and communication are emphasized through weekly stakeholder updates, maintenance of a risk register, and monthly progress reporting. Incident response is blameless, with retrospectives focusing on lessons learned and turning them into actionable improvements. Key roles—Project Manager, Product Manager, Developer, QA/Tester, Stakeholder—have well-defined responsibilities and communication touchpoints, reducing risk and ensuring process ownership.

After each sprint or release, the team holds a retrospective to analyze what worked and what didn't, tracking improvements as backlog items. This ongoing feedback loop drives continuous improvement and helps institutionalize new knowledge, benefiting both new and seasoned team members.
